# Fluency Worksheet v1 — Translating Word Problems to Linear Equations and Systems

For each problem, set up the equation(s) and solve.

1. Maria is currently 4 times as old as her son. In 12 years, she will be only twice as old as her son. How old is each of them now?

2. Two cars start 300 miles apart and drive toward each other on the same road. One travels at 50 mph and the other at 70 mph. How many hours until they meet?

3. A chemist needs to make 12 liters of a 30% acid solution by mixing a 20% acid solution with a 50% acid solution. How many liters of each should she use?

4. A piggy bank contains only nickels and dimes. There are 25 coins worth a total of $1.85. How many nickels and how many dimes are in the bank?

5. The sum of two numbers is 42, and their difference is 8. What are the two numbers?

6. A boat travels at 24 mph in still water. It goes 30 miles downstream and then 30 miles back upstream in a river whose current is 6 mph. What is the total travel time, in hours?

7. Tom is 3 years older than Jerry. The sum of their ages is 27. How old is each?

8. A theater sold 200 tickets to a play. Adult tickets cost $8 and child tickets cost $5. Total revenue was $1,375. How many adult tickets and how many child tickets were sold?
