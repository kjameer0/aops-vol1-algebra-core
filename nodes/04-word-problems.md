## What this node is

Word problems are equations written in natural language. The skill is translation: identify the unknowns, name them with variables, extract the relationships stated in the problem, write the equations, solve. AoPS Ch 3.4 emphasizes that the difficulty is almost never the algebra — it's the modeling step. This node is moderately schema-heavy: fluency in translation is needed, but the interesting problems require genuine reasoning about what equations to write.

## Fluency exercises

1. Adult tickets cost $3.25 and student tickets cost $1.75. A total of 1350 tickets were sold for $2876.50. How many of each type were sold?
2. A tank is filled by pipe A in 3 hours and drained by pipe B in 5 hours. If both are open simultaneously, how long to fill the tank from empty?
3. Two trains leave cities 400 km apart at the same time, heading toward each other. Train A travels at 90 km/h and train B at 110 km/h. When and where do they meet?
4. A 40% acid solution is mixed with a 70% acid solution to make 60 liters of a 50% solution. How many liters of each?
5. The sum of two numbers is 31 and their difference is 7. Find them.

## Schema-building exercises

1. **Modeling a new structure.** You have a two-digit number. The tens digit is twice the units digit. If you reverse the digits, the new number is 27 less than the original. Find the number. Write the equations *before* solving, and explain why each equation captures the stated relationship.
2. **Rate reasoning.** Two workers together complete a job in 4 hours. Worker A alone takes 6 hours. How long for worker B alone? Explain the structure of the rate equation: why do rates add, not times? Where does this equation come from?
3. **What changes when you change the question.** Take the ticket problem (Exercise 3-4). Now suppose you don't know the total number of tickets, only the revenue and the ratio of adult to student tickets is 2:3. Can you still solve it? Rewrite the system and solve. What condition on the given information makes a word problem solvable?

## Metacog checks

- **Explanation**: What is the systematic process for translating a word problem into equations? What are the failure modes at each step?
- **Transfer**: A boat travels 24 km upstream in 3 hours and returns in 2 hours. Find the boat's speed in still water and the current's speed. Identify which quantities become your variables and why.
- **Connection**: How does node 04 sit between systems (node 03) and quadratics (node 05)? Can you construct a word problem that requires a quadratic equation rather than a linear system? What property of the problem forces nonlinearity?
