# Goals — AoPS Vol. 1 Algebra Core

## Mastery target (extended encoding, §4)

> Given an equation of the form `u² + pu + q = 0` where `u` is itself an expression (e.g. `u = x²` or `u = x + 1/x`), identify the substitution, reduce to standard quadratic form, solve for `u`, then back-substitute to find all values of `x`. Explain *why* the substitution is valid and what structural feature of the equation signals it.

This is the schema-building peak (node 07). It requires factoring fluency, the quadratic formula, and the ability to see algebraic structure — not just execute procedures.

## Minimum bar to advance from the module (§9)

The learner can:

1. Solve any linear equation in one variable, including those requiring distribution and fraction-clearing
2. Solve `|ax + b| = c` and `|ax + b| < c` correctly — including tracking the sign flip — without arithmetic errors
3. Solve a 2×2 system by both substitution and elimination
4. Factor any quadratic `x² + bx + c` with integer roots without hesitation
5. Apply the quadratic formula to any `ax² + bx + c = 0` and simplify the result
6. Explain in plain language why completing the square produces the quadratic formula

## Ideal bar (real mastery, §9)

The learner can additionally:

1. Derive the quadratic formula from completing the square from a cold start
2. Recognize substitution opportunities in higher-degree equations and execute the full reduction
3. Explain why a system of two linear equations can have 0, 1, or infinitely many solutions — geometrically and algebraically
4. Name at least two places where quadratics appear outside algebra class and explain the connection

## Non-goals

- Memorizing the quadratic formula without being able to derive it
- The proportions chapter (AoPS Ch 4) and number theory chapter (Ch 5) — important but a separate module
- Geometry (AoPS Ch 9–18) — separate module
